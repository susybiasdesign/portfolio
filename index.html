After more than a decade collaborating with corporate marketing teams, I started my own business to focus on projects that matter to me—and to YOU! My greatest joys as a designer are figuring out the solution to a puzzle and making my clients happy. I'll work with you to determine how to best reach your audience, then deliver clean, high-quality results. If you already have ideas of your own, I'm great at translating other people's thoughts into visuals. I have simple goals as a designer: to make everything beautiful and do it with a smile.

SF SPCA Web Banner Slides

For my first freelance project with the San Francisco SPCA, I created several banner slides for their website sfspca.org — a site that gets 22,000 visitors per week. I started with client-provided photos and copy, and provided occasional photo and wording suggestions of my own. I wrote the text and came up with the conversations concept for the bunnies slide above. Cute, huh?

Locally Real Brochure

I designed this brochure for a team of real estate agents. They approached me for a trifold, but had concerns about using paper products. I offered this five-inch square bifold as an option to save paper and money, while also standing out more than a typical trifold format. The design was based off of their website's look and feel, with a little extra flair that a printed piece allows.

Unplugged Responsive Website

This seven-page website was a class project. Starting with Photoshop files for mobile, tablet, and desktop designs, I coded the HTML and CSS for a fully-responsive site with three breakpoints. The result was pixel-perfect — a goal I always strive to achieve. View the working site here.

Harris & Associates Brand Identity

As senior designer at Harris & Associates, I was responsible for the concept and execution of a brand refresh, including a new look and feel, and updated collateral and tradeshow materials. The mix and match collateral cards highlight different services and nest inside a trifold brochure that contains general company information. The pocket folder fits the collateral materials plus supplemental letter-sized documents, and has a slot for a business card.

Save the Date Wedding Invitations

A modern and fresh design for an engaged couple using their wedding colors. The logo combines the couple's names in a heart-shaped version of a yin-yang, representing their different personalities and how they complement each other in love.

Pottery Barn Kids Holiday Gift Guide

This point of purchase accordian-fold pocket guide provides handy reference for gift ideas based on category. Tabs open to gifts for boys, girls, nursery, or everyone. Each tab unfolds to a complete guide of gift ideas by product type, price, and appropriate age group.

ICF International Integrated Rebrand

At ICF International, I set the design direction for a brand refresh of the company's marketing materials with the goal of creating a uniﬁed voice among the company's 11 markets, 18 services, and numerous acquisitions. I was responsible for the concept, layout, and execution of all collateral, identity, and tradeshow materials, as well as mockups for the website design.

The Game of ESOP

I designed this board game for Harris & Associates to help teach employees about their Employee Stock Ownership Program (ESOP) in a fun, easy-to-understand format. The game is modeled after The Game of Life, and modified to relate to the company's construction and engineering industry. The box is modeled after typical board game boxes, with over-the-top family fun as the photo inspiration. (That's me with my colleagues on the box!) This project was the 2015 Society for Marketing Professional Services (SMPS) Marketing & Communications Awards Best in Show winner.

CEQA Deskbook Book Cover

I created the cover design for this comprehensive guide about complying with the California Environmental Quality Act — a must-have book for environmental professionals. Look mom, I'm on Amazon!
